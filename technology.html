Nuclear Technology: How It Works

Here we will explain the fundamentals of nuclear technology, the types of reactors, the fission process, and other basics.

Basic Principles of Nuclear Fission

Nuclear fission is the process by which the nucleus of a heavy atom splits into two or more smaller nuclei, releasing a large amount of energy. This process is harnessed in nuclear power plants to generate electricity.

When a neutron strikes a uranium-235 atom, the atom splits, releasing more neutrons that in turn strike other atoms, creating a controlled chain reaction.

Types of Nuclear Reactors

There are different designs of nuclear reactors, each with its own characteristics regarding fuel, moderator, and coolant. The most common are Pressurized Water Reactors (PWR) and Boiling Water Reactors (BWR).

These reactors are designed with multiple safety layers to prevent the release of radioactive material and ensure safe operation.
